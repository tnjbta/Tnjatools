School taught you. But were you really exam-ready?

Graduating doesn't always mean feeling ready to pass your state board exams — and there's more than one way to end up here. You're not alone, and there's no shame in any of it.

Sometimes it's the training. Cosmetology programs are built for a broad beauty license, so nails often get a small slice of the curriculum. Even dedicated nail programs vary in how much hands-on coaching and real exam practice students walk away with.

Sometimes the course was good — just fast. Accelerated and fast-track programs get you to graduation quickly, and that speed is the whole point — but a shorter runway can mean fewer practice reps, fewer full mock-exam run-throughs, and less time on the fine details. You can be happy with your course and still want that extra layer of training. Both can be true.

Sometimes time just got away. You graduated, life kept moving, and the exam kept sliding down the list. Now it's been a while — the skills feel rusty, the confidence has faded, and starting feels harder than it should. But you're not giving up, and a focused refresher is exactly how you get back on track.

And sometimes, life happened. Something outside your control pressed pause on everything — and the license had to wait. If you're back on your feet and ready to pick this dream back up, this program was built to be here when you were ready.

Whatever brought you here — you're here now. That's what matters. Walking in unprepared costs retake fees, testing delays, and months of income you can't earn until you're licensed. This program closes the gap. It's not starting over, and it's not paying twice — it's protecting everything you've already invested by making sure your last step actually lands.

Quick answers

Who is this program for?

Nail technician graduates of any Virginia school — cosmetology or nail programs — who have completed their hours but don't feel board-ready. Recent grads, fast-track and accelerated program graduates who want more practice reps, repeat examinees, and anyone whose confidence doesn't match their certificate. This program prepares you for the Virginia state board examinations.

Is this a licensure program?

No. This is an exam preparation program only. It does not award clock hours or credit, is not enrollment in a licensure program, and does not guarantee licensure. It exists to prepare you for the exams you're already eligible to take. Program content is aligned to Virginia/NIC standards.

How does the $99 registration fee work?

The $99 registration fee reserves your seat and — unlike most fees — is credited toward your program balance, so it's not extra money. Your remaining balance of $496 is due in exact cash on Day 1 as you arrive — change may not be available. You can also pay in full by card at registration, or use an Afterpay/Klarna payment plan. All payments are nonrefundable and nontransferable; any schedule change or exception is handled case by case with our team.

I graduated a while ago. Is it too late for me?

Not at all — this program was built with you in mind. Whether time got away from you or life pressed pause, if you completed your hours at a Virginia school and you're eligible to test, we'll knock the rust off, rebuild your confidence, and get you exam-ready. Tell us how long it's been on the registration form so we can target your refresher.

I've already failed an exam attempt. Is this still for me?

Especially for you. The registration form asks about your exam history and where you feel least prepared, so your coaching targets your actual weak spots — not a generic review. Many students come to us after an unsuccessful attempt.

What about my exam kit?

Kit preparation is built into the program. We review your kit against state board requirements, tell you exactly what's missing, and drill you on using it under exam conditions. Don't have a kit? Add a complete state board exam kit for $175 at registration — that's our cost, zero markup, free shipping — and it'll be verified and waiting for you on Day 1. Kit orders require a start date at least two weeks out and are nonrefundable once ordered.

When do the three days run?

Each program runs three consecutive days — Saturday, Sunday, and Monday — from 10:00 AM to 12:30 PM each day. You'll choose your preferred start date on the registration form, and we'll confirm your schedule before your program begins.

Where does the program take place?

At The Nail Juice Academy — 2715 Monticello Ave., Suite D, Norfolk, VA 23517. A Virginia DPOR-licensed nail technology school.
